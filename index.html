IGCSE Core Physics

Chapter 2 – Electronic Devices (Core Only)

Ammeter

  • Measures current in a circuit (in amperes, A).
  • Connected in series with the component.
  • Has very low resistance to avoid affecting current.
Exam Tip: Always connect an ammeter in series.

Voltmeter

  • Measures potential difference (voltage) across a component (in volts, V).
  • Connected in parallel with the component.
  • Has very high resistance to minimize current through it.
Exam Tip: Always connect a voltmeter in parallel.

Variable Resistor / Rheostat

  • Changes current in a circuit by varying resistance.
  • Used to control brightness, speed, or current.
  • Connected in series with the component.
Exam Tip: Rheostats control current by changing resistance.

Diode

  • Allows current to flow in one direction only.
  • Forward bias → current flows; Reverse bias → no current.
  • Used in rectifiers and circuit protection.
Exam Tip: Diode blocks current in reverse direction.

Diode as Rectifier

  • Converts AC to DC.
  • Single diode: half-wave rectifier.
  • Four diodes in bridge: full-wave rectifier.
  • Smooths output with capacitors (optional for Core).
Exam Tip: Rectifiers use diodes to allow only one direction of current.

LEDs and Lamps

  • LED emits light when current passes through it.
  • Lamps convert electrical energy into light (and heat).
  • LEDs must be connected correctly (polarity matters).
  • Used in indicators, displays, and lighting.
Exam Tip: LEDs are sensitive to direction and voltage.

Potentiometer / Potential Divider

  • Used to obtain variable voltage from a fixed voltage supply.
  • Three terminals: two ends of resistor, wiper in middle.
  • Used in volume controls, sensors, and calibration circuits.
Exam Tip: Potential divider gives a fraction of total voltage.
